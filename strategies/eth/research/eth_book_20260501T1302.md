## What this tells us about the strategy

1. **The flat GBM model is not a profitable signal at 30-min horizons** against Kalshi's KXETHD book. The market is at least as well calibrated as the model. Mean edge is +0.5 ¢; transaction costs (1-cent spread + Kalshi fees) eat that immediately.

2. **The systematic later-event discount (~$0.50–$1.40 below spot)** is the only persistent thing I can see. It's small and could just be funding/holding-cost premium, but worth measuring on a longer recording before discarding.

3. **Big edges live at the ATM strike during fast moves** and decay in seconds. If we want to harvest them we need a sub-second poll loop, market-data WebSocket (not REST), and post-only quoting on both sides of the ATM strike. That's a different architecture — closer to the BTC bot's MM mode than the slow-tick fair-value loop.

4. **Better vol model** would help at the ATM strike specifically. EWMA/GARCH could narrow the prob estimate around moves we're currently mis-pricing, but the gain is bounded — Kalshi's mid is already a near-optimal vol forecast.

## Suggested next moves (in rough order of leverage)

1. **Run a multi-day capture** to confirm whether the later-event spot-discount is persistent and tradable. One 5-hour clip can't tell us.
2. **Build a faster poll loop** (1 s or WebSocket) and re-run this same analysis. The 5 s cadence almost certainly under-counts genuine ATM dislocations.
3. **Try EWMA vol** in `model/volatility.py` and re-score against the same recordings — does the model beat the market once vol is regime-aware?
4. **Skip the model-vs-mid trading thesis at 30-min horizon**. If we want to trade KXETHD profitably, the edge isn't "we know vol better than Kalshi" — it's latency or microstructure.
